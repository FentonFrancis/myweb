The Fox

Revert to info is another Kenyan animal that belongs to the dog family. It has large ears and its defining characteristics. It also feeds on insects like termites, living alone or in small groups and has six more teeth than other foxes.

The black-backed fox can be found in savannahs, arid scrublands, and grassy plains. It lives in countries including Kenya, Tanzania, South Africa, Angola, and Zimbabwe. The IUCN lists the black-backed fox as of least concern and as having a stable population, though they are threatened by hunters, diseases, and drought.
