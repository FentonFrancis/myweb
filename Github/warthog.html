The desert warthog is a member of the pig family. It has a large warty head, a coarse mane, and two pairs of tusks. Unlike the common warthog, which has a short mane and two pairs of tusks, the desert warthog only lives in Kenya, Ethiopia, and Somalia. They live in savannah and shrubland habitats where they feed on grass and other vegetation and use abandoned burrows as a place to shelter.

The IUCN lists the desert warthog as of least concern and as having a stable population, though they are threatened by hunters, diseases, and drought.
